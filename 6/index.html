The Principles of Dark Noise

23 Aug 2019

At the beginning of this year I started working on my first indie app to release in the App Store called Dark Noise. It‚Äôs an ambient noise app for iPhone and iPad.

At first, I had a giant list of feature ideas and no idea how to distill them into a focused product. So I started by forcing myself to pick 3 principles to guide the design.

I eventually landed on these core principles:

  • Configurability
    • Targeting pro users who want as many options and hooks to work the app into their workflow as possible
  • Speed
    • Opening app to playing sound should be as fast as possible with no compromise since this is the 99% use case
  • Keep it dark (expect fat fingers)
    • Most users will probably be using this in a dark environment, possibly without their glasses on or half asleep. Keep that in mind.

After creating the principles, I took my list of feature ideas and tried to slot them all into one of those principles. If they didn‚Äôt fit, I tossed them in the backlog.

Keeping these principles in mind as I tried to design and form an MVP was really helpful. I have a tendency to get overly excited by an idea and drop whatever I‚Äôm doing and build it. Often that‚Äôs a bad idea, but sometimes it‚Äôs good!

Whenever I had the thought of adding a bunch of custom app icons referencing podcasts I like, I realized it was something that might actually resonate with the pro users I was targeting. And seems to have done just that!

This also played into my pricing decisions as that market tends to prefer paying up front and is a little more averse to ads. That‚Äôs not to say I won‚Äôt add a free tier in the future (in fact I probably will) but it‚Äôs something I‚Äôm always keeping in mind as I make these decisions.

Anyway, I‚Äôm not an expert at this, heck I‚Äôm barely a beginner, but maybe you‚Äôll find my thought process interesting nonetheless.
